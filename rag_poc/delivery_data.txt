Delivery Management Quality Guidelines and SOPs

Section 1: Delivery Delay Protocols
For Delivery ID 104 specifically, and generally for all construction material deliveries:
If a delivery is delayed by more than 30 minutes due to traffic, the delivery status must be automatically flagged as "At Risk".
The system should notify the site manager immediately. If the delay exceeds 60 minutes for perishable materials like ready-mix concrete, the delivery must be rejected upon arrival unless approved by a senior engineer.

Section 2: Quality Score Calculation
The quality score for concrete materials is calculated using a weighted formula:
1. Temperature at arrival (40% weight): Must be between 10°C and 32°C.
2. Slump test result (40% weight): Target slump ± 25mm.
3. Time since batching (20% weight): Must not exceed 90 minutes.
A final score below 85/100 triggers an automatic quality review by the QC team.

Section 3: Delivery Failure Criteria
A delivery is marked as "Failed" if any of the following conditions are met:
- The material arrives outside the acceptable temperature range and cannot be conditioned.
- The driver lacks mandatory safety documentation (PPE certification, site induction).
- The delivery arrives at the wrong site zone and materials are incorrectly unloaded.
- The quality score calculation yields a result below 70/100, indicating severe structural unsuitability.
